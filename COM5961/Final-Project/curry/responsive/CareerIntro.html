Career Introduction


  • The Beginning (2009-2014)

  • June 25, 2009
    Stephen Curry was selected with the seventh overall pick by the Gloden State Warriors right before New York Knicks, which Curry thought his wish was going to.

  • 2009-2011
    For the first two year of this slender boy, Stephen Curry appered 154 games with average of 18 points. He started to show his ability in scoring and three points by breaking several records tied Michael Jordan and LeBron James.
    He finished the rookie season with 166-three pointers, which were the most ever by a rookie in NBA history.

  • 2011-2012
    Due to the right ankle and foot injures, Curry missed 40 games and appeared only 26 games with 14.7 points in this year.

  • 2012-2013
    Recoved from injured, Curry carried GSW to playoffs with his teammates including another "Splash Brother" Klay Thompson, and established a new NBA single-season three-point record with 272 three-pointers and .
    Curry's 54 points against the Knicks included a career-best record 11 three-pointers, becoming the first player in NBA history to score 50-plus points while hitting 10-plus three-pointers in a game.

  • 2013-2014
    Curry appeared in 78 games, averaging career highs of 24.0 points and leading the league in three-pointers with 261.
    In February, he made his first All-Star appearance.

  • The Peak (2014-2019)

  • 2014-2015
    Scoring 23.8 points on average, Curry was voted MVP with 67 wins in GSW leading the league.
    On All-Star night, Curry won the Three-point Contest.
    Sweeping the Pelicans and beating the Grizzlies and Rockets, Curry won his first championship against Cleveland Cavaliers in the finals with the "Death Lineup".

  • 2015-2016
    Staring the season with 24-0, Curry broke his own NBA record for threes in a season, leaving the new mark at 402. He also led the Warriors with the first 73-win team in NBA history, supressing the 1995-1996 Chiacogo led by Michael Jordan.
    Scoring 30.1 points on average, Curry join the 50-40-90 club representing the shooting percentages from the field (.504), beyond the arc (.454), and the free-throw line (.908).
    He defeated the Thunder in overtime with a 0.6 seconds half-court three pointer, and tying the NBA single-game three-pointer with 12.
    In the playoffs, during the games against Rockets, Curry got injury. In the third round against the Thunders, Curry leads GSW win the series 4-3 after going down 3-1. But in the finals, GSW was defeated by the Cleveland Cavaliers after leading 3-1.

  • 2016-2018
    Continuing his three-pointers show, Curry moved into the top 10 on the NBA's career three point list on March 5, 2017.
    He also hit 13 three-pointers against New Orleans, setting an NBA record for most three-pointers made in a regular-season game.
    In the playoffs, with the help of Kevin Durant's joining, Curry and GSW won a back-to-back championships in 2017 and 2018.

  • 2018-2019
    Scoring 51 points with 11 3-pointers in only three quarters in the game with the Wizards, Curry continued showing his crazy shottings.
    Enter the playoffs with a 57-25 record, Curry and his teammates got injury on after another. In the finals, GSW got a loss of 2-4 against Toronto Raptors.
